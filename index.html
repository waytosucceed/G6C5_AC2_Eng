Pronouns


Personal Pronouns:

Personal pronouns refer to a person, animal, place, or thing.

  • I will see you tomorrow.
  • Give these books to them.
Possessive Pronouns:

Possessive pronouns indicate possession.

  • Is this your pen? No, it's mine.
  • The book is hers; she lent it to me yesterday.
Demonstrative Pronouns:

Demonstrative pronouns point out or refer to a person, place, or thing.

  • This is my favorite book.
  • That is my favorite book.
  • These are the keys to the car.
  • Those are the houses we looked at yesterday.
Relative Pronouns:

Relative pronouns introduce relative clauses.

  • She is the student whose artwork won the competition.
  • I know someone who can fix your car.
Reflexive Pronouns:

Reflexive pronouns reflect the actions of the subject.

  • They enjoyed themselves at the party.
  • I locked myself inside the house.
Indefinite Pronouns:

Indefinite pronouns refer to unspecified persons or things.

  • Everyone should bring their lunch to the picnic.
  • You can choose anything from the menu.
Interrogative Pronouns:

Interrogative pronouns are used to inquire about something or someone.

  • Who is your best friend?
  • Which is your favorite subject?
